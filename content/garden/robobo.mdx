Robobo is my Systems Design Engineering capstone: a social robot meant to be
affordable enough that an older adult living alone could actually have one. It
sits at the intersection of firmware, cloud, and physical design — which is
exactly the boundary I like working at.

## System overview

The robot is really three subsystems that have to agree on timing:

1. **Voice & language.** Amazon Alexa captures intent and hands it to a custom
   AWS Lambda skill; OpenAI generates an emotion-aware response.
2. **Expression.** A Raspberry Pi 5 turns that response into a "mood," and an
   ESP32 drives the servos and LCD face so the robot physically reacts.
3. **Mechanical.** The enclosure and linkages are modelled in SolidWorks around
   off-the-shelf servos to keep cost down.

## Engineering decisions

- **Split the brain from the body.** The Pi does the heavy, non-real-time work
  (network, language); the ESP32 owns the hard-real-time motion. They talk over
  a simple serial protocol so neither blocks the other.
- **Design for cost from day one.** Every part choice was checked against a
  target bill of materials — the affordability constraint is the point, not an
  afterthought.

## Open questions I'm still working through

- How expressive can servo + LCD be before it feels uncanny?
- Where should the emotion model live long-term — cloud, or on-device?
